The goal of this project is to develop an interactive, web-based visual application showcasing a real-world dataset, namely US unemployment through the ongoing COVID pandemic.The primary sources of data are the unemployment insurance weekly claims data from the US Department of Labor and labor force statistics from the current population survey (CPS) of the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics as listed below.

  • State level unemployment data from 2015 - Present (Weekly) - https://oui.doleta.gov/unemploy/claims.asp
  • Demographic level unemployment data (Monthly) - https://download.bls.gov/pub/time.series/ln/
  • Industry level employment data (Monthly) - https://download.bls.gov/pub/time.series/ce/

Who is counted as unemployed?

  • All those who did not have a job at all during the survey reference week, made at least one specific active effort to find a job during the prior 4 weeks, and were available for work (unless temporarily ill)
  • All those who were not working and were waiting to be called back to a job from which they had been laid off. (They need not be looking for work to be classified as unemployed)
  • The official unemployment rate for the nation is the number of unemployed as a percentage of the labor force (the sum of the employed and unemployed)
